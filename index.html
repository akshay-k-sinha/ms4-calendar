About
Built for medical students,
by people who get it.

Fourth year should be exciting — not stressful. But between ERAS, Step 2, interviews, and rotations, most students end up buried in spreadsheets, sticky notes, and GroupMe reminders.

We built MatchReady because our friends deserved better. One place for everything — rotations, interviews, deadlines, letters — that actually looks good and works on every device.

No ads. No data selling. Your data is yours — always private, always exportable. Just a tool that helps you get to Match Day with your sanity intact.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 20, 2026

What We Collect

When you sign in with Google, we receive your name, email address, and profile photo from your Google account. We use this solely to identify your account and display your profile in the app. We do not request access to your Google Calendar, Gmail, or any other Google services beyond basic profile information.

What You Store

All schedule data — rotations, interviews, key dates, letters of recommendation, and notes — is entered by you and stored in your personal account. We do not access, read, analyze, or sell your schedule data. Your data is never used for advertising, profiling, or shared with third parties.

MatchReady does not collect Protected Health Information (PHI), medical records, patient data, payment card numbers, Social Security numbers, or any sensitive personal information beyond your Google profile. Do not enter any such information into the app.

Your data is stored in Google Cloud Firestore, hosted on Google Cloud Platform infrastructure in the United States. You can delete all your data at any time from the My Account page using the "Clear All Data" option. This permanently removes all your schedule data from our servers. We do not use cookies or any tracking technologies. We use your browser's localStorage to cache your schedule data for faster loading and to remember your theme preference (light/dark mode). This data stays on your device and is not sent to any server.
